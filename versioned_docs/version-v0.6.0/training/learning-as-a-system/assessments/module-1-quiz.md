## Section A: Scenario Questions (1 point each)

**Q1.** A team member studies a new process extensively for two days before a project kick-off meeting. She explains it fluently in the meeting. Three weeks later, when applying it, she cannot recall the steps. Which learning system concept best explains this?

- A) She has low motivation stock and stopped caring after the meeting
- B) Knowledge stock spiked during study, but the high forgetting outflow drained it without retrieval practice to maintain it
- C) Her skill stock was inadequate from the start
- D) The B1 gap-closing loop collapsed after the meeting

---

**Q2.** A developer practices a new coding pattern for 10 minutes every day for three weeks. Another developer practices for 3 hours in one intensive session. Both invested approximately equal total time. After three weeks, who has the higher durable skill stock?

- A) The developer who practiced intensively — they invested more focus per session
- B) The developer who practiced daily — consistent low-intensity inflows accumulate more stock against the forgetting outflow
- C) They are equal — total time determines total stock
- D) The intensive developer in the short term, the daily developer in the long term

---

**Q3.** A junior team member makes an error, receives critical feedback in front of the team, and becomes visibly withdrawn. She stops asking questions and avoids difficult tasks for the next two weeks. What is the most accurate ST diagnosis?

- A) She lacks sufficient knowledge stock to perform the tasks
- B) The critical public feedback drained her confidence stock → motivation stock reduced → effort reduced → skill growth stalled (vicious R1 loop activated)
- C) The B1 gap-closing loop reached equilibrium
- D) Her learning inflows were insufficient before the incident

---

**Q4.** A team's knowledge gap on a new technology is large. The team leader assigns three members to intensive research for a week. By the end of the week, the gap is significantly smaller, and the team's study time naturally reduces. Which loop is primarily responsible for the reduced study time?

- A) R1 — the competence loop has activated and is sustaining learning
- B) B1 — the gap-closing loop has reduced the pressure that was driving effort
- C) A new balancing loop created by the team leader's assignment
- D) The forgetting outflow has increased, counteracting the gains

---

**Q5.** Which of the following is a **stock** in a personal learning system?

- A) The number of documentation pages read per week
- B) The frequency of practice sessions
- C) The accumulated confidence in performing a skill — a quantity that exists right now, built over time
- D) The rate at which new concepts are being studied

---

**Q6.** A student feels he understands a topic completely after reading the chapter. He takes a practice test and scores 40%. Which delay explains this gap?

- A) The effort-before-mastery delay: knowledge stock rose from reading, but skill stock (ability to apply it) has not yet accumulated through practice
- B) The B1 gap-closing loop collapsed the knowledge stock after reading
- C) The R1 competence loop ran in reverse during the test
- D) The forgetting outflow ran faster than the inflow during the test

---

**Q7.** A team member spends 2 hours re-reading notes she made last month. Her test score the next day is only slightly better than before she re-read. What does this suggest about her learning approach?

- A) Her knowledge stock has decayed past recovery
- B) Re-reading is a passive inflow with a high forgetting outflow — it builds knowledge stock less effectively than retrieval practice
- C) She needs more total study hours, not a different method
- D) The B1 gap-closing loop has reached equilibrium

---

**Q8.** An organization pays for employees to attend a two-day training workshop. Six months later, managers observe almost no change in work behavior. What is the most likely structural explanation?

- A) The training content was irrelevant to the employees' work
- B) Employees lacked motivation to apply what they learned
- C) The training created a one-time knowledge inflow with no retrieval practice or on-the-job application to maintain the stock — forgetting outflow drained the gains before the behavior changed
- D) The training was too advanced for the employees' existing knowledge stock

## Section B: Short Answer (1 point each)

**Q9.** You are helping a colleague who has been trying to learn data analysis for 3 months without visible progress. She studies for 2–3 hours every Saturday. Describe in 3–5 sentences:
- What stocks and flows are operating in her learning system?
- Which structural issue is most likely causing the lack of progress?
- One structural change you would recommend and why it would help (reference the specific stock or flow it affects)

**Q10.** Draw or describe in words a Causal Loop Diagram with:
- The R1 Competence Loop (virtuous version)
- The B1 Gap-Closing Loop
- At least one delay marked

Name each loop and indicate the direction of each causal link (+/−).

## Answer Key

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers (attempt all questions first)</summary>

**Q1. Correct: B**  
The knowledge stock spiked during the two-day study session but was never reinforced by retrieval practice. Forgetting outflow ran continuously for three weeks. Without review, the knowledge stock decayed below the retrieval threshold by the time she needed it for application.

**Q2. Correct: B**  
Daily practice distributes inflows across time, continuously topping up the stock before significant forgetting occurs. Intensive single-session practice builds a high-peak stock that decays rapidly — like a leaky bucket filled all at once vs. refilled regularly. Equal total time with distributed intervals produces more durable accumulation.

**Q3. Correct: B**  
The public critical feedback directly reduced confidence stock. Low confidence → reduced motivation → effort drops → less skill growth. This is the vicious version of the R1 Competence Loop activating. The structural intervention is to protect the confidence stock: private feedback, framing error as a gap to address rather than a judgment of capability, and engineering early wins to restart the virtuous direction.

**Q4. Correct: B**  
The B1 (Gap-Closing) loop: large knowledge gap → high effort → gap reduces → effort reduces naturally. This is the stabilizing behavior of a balancing loop reaching equilibrium. The reduced study time is not failure — it is the balancing loop doing its job. The risk is: R1 doesn't activate, and the team stops before reaching mastery.

**Q5. Correct: C**  
A stock is a quantity measurable at a point in time. Accumulated confidence is a stock — it exists in a certain amount right now and changes gradually. A, B, and D are all **rates** (flows), not stocks.

**Q6. Correct: A**  
The deceptive competence delay: knowledge stock rose from reading, but skill stock (the ability to apply the knowledge on a test) requires practice to accumulate. The student confused the two stocks. Knowledge stock being high does not mean skill stock is high. They are different stocks with different inflows — study builds knowledge stock; practice builds skill stock.

**Q7. Correct: B**  
Re-reading is a passive inflow. It processes the material again, but because there is no retrieval effort, the memory trace is not significantly strengthened and the forgetting outflow rate remains high. Switching to retrieval practice (trying to recall without the notes, then checking) would reduce the outflow rate and produce more durable stock.

**Q8. Correct: C**  
The training created a one-time knowledge inflow. Without follow-up retrieval practice, peer discussion, on-the-job application, or spaced review sessions, the forgetting outflow drained the gains before they could translate to behavioral change. This is the most common failure mode of event-based training. The structural fix is to build the retrieval and application flows into the weeks after the workshop, not just the workshop itself.

**Q9.** Full credit requires:
- At least one stock (knowledge/skill/confidence) and one flow (inflow or outflow) named
- A specific structural diagnosis (likely: weekly cramming = low-frequency inflow with high forgetting outflow; or: passive study with high forgetting outflow)
- A structural recommendation that explicitly addresses the identified stock or flow (e.g., "switch from weekly cramming to 20-minute daily retrieval practice — this distributes inflows and reduces the net forgetting outflow, building more durable knowledge stock")

**Q10.** Full credit requires:
- R1: at least 4 variables in a closed loop, all positive links, labeled %% R1
  Example: Skill → (+) → Success → (+) → Confidence → (+) → Motivation → (+) → Effort → (+) → Skill
- B1: gap and effort in a closed loop, with the gap link negative (gap → effort: positive; effort → gap: negative)
  Example: Knowledge Gap → (+) → Learning Effort → (−) → Knowledge Gap (closing)
- At least one delay marked, with a note explaining what the delay represents (e.g., "delay between effort and mastery becoming visible")
