# Module 3 Portfolio Assessment — Emotional Intelligence as Systems Leadership

**Program**: Mastering Human Emotions through Systems Thinking  
**Level**: L3 Mastery  
**Format**: Portfolio submission (4 evidence items)  
**Prerequisite**: Module 2 quiz passed + Exercise 2.2 submitted  
**Issue**: [#27](https://github.com/imranypatel/xp-org1/issues/27)

---

## Overview

L3 assessment is portfolio-based. You demonstrate mastery by submitting evidence of
applied systemic emotional intelligence across real professional contexts — not a written
exam. This reflects the L3 principle: mastery is demonstrated through *changing the system's
capacity to understand itself*, not through answering questions about concepts.

---

## Required Evidence Items

### Evidence Item 1 — Full ST Emotional Analysis

**What**: A written analysis of a real team event, interpersonal dynamic, or organisational
situation using the full 8-step Emotional ST Framework.

**Requirements**:
- Event must be real (not hypothetical)
- Analysis must cover all 8 steps: Stocks, Flows, Loops, Delays, Archetypes, Leverage, Side Effects, Monitoring
- Must include a Causal Loop Diagram with at least two interacting loops (text-based Mermaid notation accepted)
- Must identify the Meadows leverage level of the proposed intervention
- Length: 800–1500 words

**Evaluation criteria**: See Competency Rubric dimensions 1–5.

---

### Evidence Item 2 — Second Domain Analysis

**What**: A second full analysis from a *different context* than Evidence Item 1.

**Why different context**: L3 mastery means the framework generalises. Applying it once
is practitioner-level. Applying it across domains with equal clarity is mastery-level.

**Examples of domain variety**:
- Evidence 1: team retrospective dynamic → Evidence 2: cross-department negotiation
- Evidence 1: 1:1 coaching conversation → Evidence 2: product launch review emotional climate
- Evidence 1: onboarding experience → Evidence 2: team reorganisation response

**Requirements**: Same as Evidence Item 1.

---

### Evidence Item 3 — Facilitation Record

**What**: Notes, a brief write-up, or a structured account of a real facilitated session
(retrospective, conflict resolution, or team alignment conversation) using the ST framing.

**Requirements**:
- The session must have actually happened (not a simulation)
- Write-up must describe: the opening framing used, how ST vocabulary was introduced,
  what loop or archetype was named during the session, and the structural intervention
  or commitment agreed upon
- Include your assessment of what worked, what the delay in effect is expected to be,
  and how you plan to monitor whether the intervention is effective
- Length: 400–800 words

---

### Evidence Item 4 — Coaching Record

**What**: A written account of a reframing coaching conversation with a peer or team
member in which you applied the Reframing Sequence from Module 3 Part 4.

**Requirements**:
- The conversation must have actually happened
- Write-up must cover: the presenting emotional state, the stock you named together,
  the flow you identified, the loop you surfaced, and the leverage point discussed
- Include the peer's response and your assessment of whether the reframing was received
- Include a note on what you would do differently in the next conversation
- Length: 300–600 words

## Competency Rubric

All evidence items are evaluated against five dimensions. The portfolio pass requires
**Proficient or Mastery on all five dimensions** for at least two of the four evidence items.

| Dimension | Developing | Proficient | Mastery |
|-----------|------------|-----------|---------|
| **1. Structural Diagnosis** | Attributes cause to individuals; uses personality or motivation explanations | Correctly identifies structural drivers; avoids individual blame; names the system | Diagnoses multiple interacting structures simultaneously; identifies which is primary |
| **2. Loop Identification** | Names a loop but polarity incorrect or loop incomplete | Correctly draws and labels loops with correct polarity; distinguishes R and B | Identifies multiple interacting loops; explains how their interaction produces the observed dynamic |
| **3. Leverage Point** | Intervention is behavioural prescription; no structural change | Proposes structural intervention at a named Meadows level; explains the mechanism | Evaluates multiple leverage options; selects highest-leverage with explicit reasoning about why lower levels are insufficient |
| **4. Delay Accounting** | No mention of delays; assumes immediate effects | Identifies at least one significant delay; explains how it affects diagnosis or recovery | Designs intervention with explicit delay management; specifies monitoring signals and their timing |
| **5. Side Effect Analysis** | No consideration of unintended consequences | Identifies at least one side effect; proposes mitigation | Maps side effects across multiple stocks; designs intervention to minimise negative side effects while preserving benefits |

## Submission and Review Process

1. **Compile** all four evidence items into a single document or linked GitHub Gist
2. **Submit** to your team lead with the subject line: `[L3 Portfolio] Human Emotions ST — [Your Name]`
3. **Review meeting**: Your team lead will schedule a 30-minute review conversation.
   This is not a defence — it is a conversation about your analyses, the leverage choices you made,
   and your facilitation and coaching experiences
4. **Feedback**: You will receive written feedback on each evidence item within one week
5. **If not yet passing**: You may revise and resubmit evidence items that scored Developing.
   Items scored Proficient or Mastery do not need revision
