## Part A — Multiple Choice (10 pts each)

### Q1 — Stocks vs Events

During a team meeting, a manager interrupts a team member mid-sentence to correct them in
front of the group. Immediately after the meeting, the team member continues their work
normally. Two weeks later, however, they stop volunteering ideas in meetings.

From a Systems Thinking perspective, the two-week delay is best explained by:

**A)** The team member is a slow processor  
**B)** Trust is an event that dissipated after the meeting  
**C)** Trust is a stock that drained gradually; the behavioural change occurred when the stock reached a critical threshold  
**D)** The team member is choosing to be passive-aggressive

*Answer*: **C** — Trust drains over time; behaviour changes when the stock reaches a low enough level that protective behaviour (silence) becomes the system output.

---

### Q2 — Inflows and Outflows

Which of the following is correctly classified as an **inflow** to the Psychological Safety stock?

**A)** A team member receives blame after a publicly visible mistake  
**B)** A team member asks a "basic" question and is told "you should already know this"  
**C)** A manager says "great catch — this is exactly the kind of thing we need to surface"  
**D)** A retrospective is cancelled because leadership wants to move forward

*Answer*: **C** — Recognition for surfacing a problem is a direct inflow to Psychological Safety.

---

### Q3 — Reinforcing Loops

A team member gets positive feedback on their first contribution to a cross-functional
project. They feel more confident and volunteer for a second, more complex contribution.
That also goes well, and their reputation grows.

This is an example of:

**A)** A balancing loop correcting toward equilibrium  
**B)** A reinforcing loop creating a virtuous cycle  
**C)** An escalation archetype  
**D)** A Tragedy of the Commons

*Answer*: **B** — Competence → success → confidence → more effort → more competence. Classic reinforcing (virtuous) loop.

---

### Q4 — Delays

After a significant conflict between two team members, the team lead calls a resolution
meeting. The meeting goes well; both parties express understanding. Three days later, one
of the members makes a sarcastic comment in a group chat that triggers a new argument.

The most structurally accurate explanation is:

**A)** The resolution was insincere  
**B)** The emotional regulation loop (B1) requires more time than 3 days to refill depleted trust stocks  
**C)** The team member deliberately sabotaged the resolution  
**D)** Conflict is inevitable and not structurally manageable

*Answer*: **B** — The balancing regulation loop has a delay. Trust has not fully refilled in 3 days; the team is still operating on depleted stocks.

---

### Q5 — The Fear Spiral

A team begins using a new project management tool. Mistakes made in the tool are publicly
visible to leadership. Team members become reluctant to experiment with features. The tool
is used only for basic logging — its more powerful features remain unused. Over time, the
tool is blamed for being "not useful."

Identify the loop operating:

**A)** B1 — Emotional Regulation  
**B)** R1 — Trust Spiral (virtuous)  
**C)** R2 — Fear Spiral (vicious) — reversed Trust Spiral  
**D)** B2 — Social Mirror

*Answer*: **C** — Fear of visible mistakes → reduced experimentation → reduced capability → worse outcomes → more fear. Vicious reinforcing loop.

---

### Q6 — Anxiety Stock

Which structural change is most likely to reduce an inflow to the Anxiety stock?

**A)** Telling team members to "think positively"  
**B)** Publishing a clear weekly update that addresses open questions and upcoming changes  
**C)** Replacing the team lead  
**D)** Holding more frequent check-in meetings without an agenda

*Answer*: **B** — Clarity and predictability directly reduce the ambiguity inflow to Anxiety. Agendaless meetings and exhortations are behavioural prescriptions that do not change the structural flow.

---

### Q7 — Escalation Archetype

Two teams, Team Alpha and Team Beta, share a dependency. Team Alpha submitted a change
without notifying Team Beta; Beta's release broke. Beta's manager sent a sharp email
to Alpha. Alpha's manager sent an even sharper response. Now neither team submits changes
without a lengthy review process that slows both teams down.

Which archetype is operating?

**A)** Shifting the Burden  
**B)** Tragedy of the Commons  
**C)** Escalation  
**D)** Success to the Successful

*Answer*: **C** — Each party's defensive response increases the other's threat perception → Escalation.

---

### Q8 — Social Mirror

A team lead consistently opens retrospectives with "What went badly?" without framing.
Team members consistently report problems without proposed solutions. The retrospective
becomes a complaint session; action items rarely get created.

Which loop is this?

**A)** R1 — Trust Spiral  
**B)** B2 — Social Mirror  
**C)** B1 — Emotional Regulation  
**D)** R2 — Fear Spiral

*Answer*: **B** — The opening question sets the emotional register (problem focus); the Social Mirror amplifies it through participant responses; the pattern is self-reinforcing through the balancing loop.

## Part B — Short Answer (10 pts each)

### Q9 — Emotional Stock vs Emotional Event

Explain the difference between an emotional *event* and an emotional *stock*. Give one
example of each from a workplace context. Why does this distinction matter for understanding
persistent team dynamics?

*Scoring guide*: Full marks require: correct definition of both terms, distinct examples,
and an explanation of why the stock framing matters for diagnosis or intervention.

### Q10 — Delay Consequences

A team successfully completes a difficult project. The team lead gives verbal appreciation
in the closing meeting. Three weeks later, two team members resign, citing feeling
undervalued. The team lead is surprised — "We celebrated the project!"

Using the concept of delays and stocks, explain how this outcome is structurally predictable.
What should the team lead have done differently?

*Scoring guide*: Full marks require: reference to the Enthusiasm/Trust stock, identification
of the delay, explanation of why a single event is insufficient to maintain stock levels,
and a structural recommendation (not a behavioural one).
