## Why This Training Exists

Emotions shape every meeting, every decision, every collaboration. Yet in most professional
settings they are treated as unwelcome disruptions — things to suppress, manage privately,
or push aside in the interest of "professionalism." This framing is not only wrong; it is
structurally costly.

Emotions are system signals. They are the outputs of feedback loops, the result of stocks
being filled or depleted, and the drivers of behaviour that either amplify or dampen
performance. When a team member feels anxious in a meeting, that anxiety is not a character
flaw — it is a level in a stock that has been filling due to identifiable inflows (ambiguity,
overload, perceived threat). When trust collapses in a team, it does not happen because
people are bad — it happens because a reinforcing loop reversed direction.

This training gives team members the Systems Thinking vocabulary to *read* emotional dynamics
structurally, diagnose root causes, and intervene at high-leverage points — rather than
reacting to symptoms or suppressing signals.

> **The core insight**: Emotions are not the opposite of reason — they are data. A team that
> can read its own emotional stocks and flows can intervene before vicious cycles solidify,
> build trust as a structural asset rather than a hoped-for atmosphere, and sustain the
> psychological safety that makes learning and role performance possible.

## Program Structure

Three progressive levels. Each builds on the previous. Level-up requires both a quiz pass
**and** practical application evidence.

```
L1 Emotions as a System   →   L2 Emotional Dynamics    →   L3 Systems Leadership
     (3–4 hours)                in Practice (6–8 hrs)       (10–12 hrs + portfolio)
  ST Foundations L1            L1 quiz ≥ 70%             L2 pass + 2 applied analyses
```

---

### Level 1 — Emotions as a System

**Goal**: Build the mental model that emotions are stocks and flows — not events — and that
their dynamics follow the same structural rules as any other system. Diagnose a real
emotional pattern using ST vocabulary.

**Who needs it**: Every team member. L1 is the foundation for reading team dynamics and
personal emotional patterns through a structural lens.

**Learning outcomes**:
- Name at least four emotional stocks and describe their key inflows and outflows
- Draw a simple causal loop diagram (CLD) of one emotional feedback loop
- Distinguish an emotional event (trigger) from an emotional stock (level)
- Identify one delay in an emotional dynamic and explain its consequences
- Apply one ST archetype (Escalation or Shifting the Burden) to a workplace scenario

**Module**: [Module 1 — Emotions as a System](./module-1-introduction.md)  
**Assessment**: [Module 1 Quiz](./assessments/module-1-quiz.md)

---

### Level 2 — Emotional Dynamics in Practice

**Goal**: Apply ST tools to real emotional dynamics in teams — identify archetypes, draw
CLDs, and find leverage points. Move from diagnosis to intervention design.

**Who needs it**: Team members who regularly collaborate, lead discussions, or manage
relationships across roles. Required before L3.

**Learning outcomes**:
- Draw a multi-loop CLD covering at least two interacting emotional stocks
- Identify the Escalation, Shifting the Burden, and Tragedy of the Commons archetypes
  in a realistic team scenario
- Propose a structural intervention (not a behavioural prescription) for a vicious loop
- Describe how delays cause misdiagnosis of emotional root causes
- Apply the Social Mirror balancing loop to a communication challenge

**Module**: [Module 2 — Emotional Dynamics in Practice](./module-2-practitioner.md)  
**Assessment**: [Module 2 Quiz](./assessments/module-2-quiz.md)

---

### Level 3 — Emotional Intelligence as Systems Leadership

**Goal**: Lead the team's emotional system. Design structural interventions for psychological
safety, model systemic emotional intelligence in real issues and discussions, and build
organisational capacity to sustain virtuous emotional loops.

**Who needs it**: Team leads, principal advisors, and any member aspiring to take on
cross-domain or orchestration roles.

**Learning outcomes**:
- Facilitate a blameless retrospective framed in ST vocabulary
- Design a structural norm (rule, practice, or process) that shifts a team emotional archetype
- Analyse a real GitHub issue or team event using the full emotional ST framework (stocks,
  flows, loops, delays, archetypes, leverage)
- Coach a peer through reframing an emotional reaction as system feedback
- Present a portfolio demonstrating applied emotional ST across at least two domains

**Module**: [Module 3 — Emotional Intelligence as Systems Leadership](./module-3-mastery.md)  
**Assessment**: [Module 3 Portfolio Rubric](./assessments/module-3-quiz.md)

## Key References

| Author | Work | Why Relevant |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Donella Meadows | *Thinking in Systems* (2008) | Stocks, flows, feedback loops — the foundational vocabulary |
| Daniel Goleman | *Emotional Intelligence* (1995) | Introduces the concept of emotional competencies in professional settings |
| Paul Ekman | *Emotions Revealed* (2003) | Taxonomy of emotions and their triggers — useful for naming stocks precisely |
| Peter Senge | *The Fifth Discipline* (1990) | Organisational learning, mental models, and the emotional climate of learning organisations |
| Amy Edmondson | *The Fearless Organization* (2018) | Psychological safety as a structural condition — the trust stock in practice |
